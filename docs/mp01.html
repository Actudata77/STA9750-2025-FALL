Introduction

In this report, we will take a close look at Netflix’s global Top 10 viewership dataset. Our ultimate goal is to uncover key insights into user engagement with the films and TV shows, evaluate patterns and highlight opportunities for growth. In the first part of the report, by using some some Explanatory Data Analysis questions, I uncover some answers about targeted questions on Netflix most popular domestic and international programs. Those findings are then translated in press releases in the second part of report.

Some key facts:

  • Netflix operates in more than 90 countries.
  • Most of Netflix revenues are from international markets.
  • Global impact of programs such as Squid Game, Stranger Things and KPop Demon Hunters.
  • How local content shows growth potential.

The column names such as weekly_hours_viewed are not properly formatted. Large numbers in that column are also not presented with commas. Let’s fix that!

Show code 
library(stringr)
format_titles <- function(df) {
  colnames(df) <- colnames(df) |>
    str_replace_all("_", " ") |>
    str_to_lower() |>          # first make all letters lowercase
    str_to_title()             # then title-case properly
  df
}

GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  format_titles() |>
  head(n = 20) |>
  datatable(options = list(searching = FALSE, info = FALSE)) |>
  formatRound(c("Weekly Hours Viewed", "Weekly Views"))

We can now drop the season_title since here we are only showing films.

Show code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  select(-season_title) |>
  format_titles() |>
  head(n = 20) |>
  datatable(options = list(searching = FALSE, info = FALSE)) |>
  formatRound(c("Weekly Hours Viewed", "Weekly Views"))

Finally, let’s convert Runtime to minutes

Show code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>

    -season_title,

8.The movie Red Notice has a runtime of 1 hour and 58 minutes. Approximately how many views did it receive in 2021? Note that Netflix does not provide the weekly_views values that far back in the past, but you can compute it yourself using the total view time and the runtime.

Show code 
red_notice_views_2021 <- GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  filter(show_title == "Red Notice" & year(week) == 2021) |>
  summarise(total_views = sum(weekly_hours_viewed) / (1 + 58 / 60)) |>

9.How many Films reached Number 1 in the US but did not originally debut there? That is, find films that first appeared on the Top 10 chart at, e.g., Number 4 but then became more popular and eventually hit Number 1? What is the most recent film to pull this off?

Show code 
library(dplyr)
library(stringr)

# Holder
us_films <- COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  filter(country_name == "United States", str_starts(category, "Films")) %>%
  select(show_title, week, weekly_rank)

debut <- us_films |>
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(debut_week = min(week, na.rm = TRUE)) |>
  ungroup()

# get the ranks
films_us_number1 <- us_films |>
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(
    best_rank   = min(weekly_rank, na.rm = TRUE),
    last_week_1 = max(week[weekly_rank == 1], na.rm = TRUE)
  ) |>
  ungroup() |>
  inner_join(debut, by = "show_title") |>
  left_join(us_films, by = c("show_title", "debut_week" = "week")) |>
  rename(debut_rank = weekly_rank) |>
  # different start but eventually hit # 1
  filter(!is.na(debut_rank), debut_rank > 1, best_rank == 1) |>
  arrange(desc(last_week_1)) |> 
  format_titles() |> 
  datatable(options = list(searching = FALSE, info = FALSE))

films_us_number1

Press Releases

Press Release 1:

Stranger Things: A Global Phenomenon Returns for Final Season

According to a 2025 Collider ranking, Season 4 of Stranger Things is widely regarded as the strongest of the first four seasons. This sets the tone for the highly anticipated final season arriving later this year. Stranger Things has really proven to be a truly global phenomenon. In the United States alone, it held a place in the Top 10 for 16 weeks, while internationally it achieved even greater: 24 weeks in Ukraine, 23 weeks in Pakistan, 21 weeks in India, and more than a dozen weeks across European countries like Belgium and France. It also showed success in African countries like Kenya and Nigeria. Compared to other popular English-language TV shows such as Wednesday and The Crown, Stranger Things remains one of Netflix’s most dominant hits. Globally it is surpassed only by Squid Game. With nearly 3 billion hours viewed worldwide, excitement for the last season is huge.

Press Release 2:

Netflix breakthrough in India

India is the world most populated country and has quickly become on of Netflix fastest growing market. TV shows and films in Hindi language continue their dominance in the country’s top 10.Local TV and shows have stayed in the country’s top 10 for numerous weeks. This prove the show broadest appeal to the domestic audience.

“The Great Indian Kapil Show” remained in the India’s Top 10 for 43 weeks, outperforming other international competitors. In the meantime ,several of the most successful domestic TV shows and Films have never appeared in the US Top 10. This demonstrates the value of programming specific to different regions in the world. With Netflix counting a lot on the success of international markets, India remains a key driver of the company’s success at the international level.As Netflix continue to rely on international market, these results reaffirm the platform continued investment in India.

Press Release 3

Netflix Announces New Africa Originals Hub in Nigeria

Nigeria is the most populous African country and home to Africa’s largest film industry (Nollywood) with a booming youth demographic. With more than 60% of its population under the age of 30, Nigeria represents one of the youngest, most dynamic entertainment markets in the world. The new generation generation, more tech savvy,is driving rapid growth both within Africa and abroad.

In recent years, African productions such as Queen Sono (South Africa), Aníkúlápó (Nigeria), and Blood & Water (South Africa) have achieved strong engagement, demonstrating the rising demand for African storytelling. Data on viewership shows that Netflix members outside Africa are turning more and more to African films and series, with growth in hours viewed nearly doubling year over year.

“Our goal is simple: to help Africa’s incredible storytellers share their voices with the world,” said Ted Sarandos, Co-CEO of Netflix. “Nigeria’s young, creative, and connected population is shaping the future of global storytelling, and we are proud to be investing where the next wave of cultural influence begins. This initiative emphasizes Netflix’s long-term commitment to Africa as both a source of creative innovation and a fast growing market, anchored by Nigeria’s youth-driven demand for excellent storytelling.

Conclusion

Netflix’s Top 10 data reveals a balance between global blockbusters and the rise of local storytelling. While hits like Stranger Things and Squid Game dominate worldwide, markets such as India and Nigeria show how regional content and young audiences are shaping the future for Netflix. Together, these trends show Netflix’s dual strategy: continue to invest in global hits while also strengthening local productions for a sustained growth in diverse markets.
